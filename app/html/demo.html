The following are screen shots of a soon to be released early demonstration of Map of Life that provides initial and very simple, core functionality. In this first demo release, Map of Life will provide the means to search on a common or scientific name and get a list of results based on the Catalog of Life database (http://www.catalogueoflife.org/). A user can select to display a set of available expert opinion range maps based on their results set. A user may use familar mapping controls to zoom in and out to view the range map. That same user can also choose to examine other kinds of distributional knowledge about where taxa are found; for example point occurrences from species repositories such as VertNet, or areas where species have been surveyed. Stay tuned for the working demonstration!
Range Maps: One of the primary forms of data in MOL is the species range map. The project will be developing new ways for exploring and visualizing this data on the web.
Search: The diversity of data MOL plans to make available for mapping and analysis will require new methods of accessing this data through searching and filtering data.
A big part of MOL will be a web-interface to access maps, analysis results, and explore data. We are building much of this user interface and client technology from the ground up.
